Marketing Campaigns Analysis

Optimizing campaigns through customer insights & performance metrics

Project Overview

Marketing campaigns are powerful tools for customer engagement — but understanding who responds and why is key to maximizing ROI. In this project, I analyzed a multi-channel marketing dataset to answer crucial questions: Which channels drive the most conversions? What demographics respond best? How should future budgets be allocated?

Dataset Details

The dataset from Marketing_campaigns.xlsx includes:

  • Customer demographics: Age, income, marital status, education level
  • Campaign data: Number of offers sent, channels used (email, web, social media, store)
  • Purchasing behavior: Total spend, frequency of purchases, product categories
  • Response metrics: Whether customers accepted each campaign

This data enables segmentation analysis and reveals channel performance across different audiences.

Analytical Approach

The workflow combined Power Query for data preparation and DAX for advanced metrics:

  • Data cleaning: Handled missing demographics, standardized categorical fields (e.g., education levels).
  • Feature engineering: Derived KPIs such as total campaign responses, average spend per channel, and conversion rates.
  • DAX calculations: Created measures like ROI per campaign, response % by age group, and cumulative customer lifetime value (CLV).
  • Dashboard design: Used interactive filters for age ranges, channels, and campaign periods to allow drill-down exploration.

Key Insights

Best Performing Channel: Email campaigns
Highest conversion rate (~30%)
Demographic Sweet Spot: Age 30–45, middle-income
Strongest engagement & repeat purchases
Campaign Seasonality: End-of-year promotions
Spikes in November–December
Optimization Opportunity: Social media
Low ROI, needs targeting refinement

Business Impact

By leveraging these insights, marketers can allocate budgets to the most effective channels, personalize campaigns for responsive segments, and boost ROI by over 20% in subsequent campaigns.
